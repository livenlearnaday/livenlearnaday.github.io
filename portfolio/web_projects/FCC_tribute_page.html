A dedicated inventor.

Timeline:

  • 11 Feb 1847 - Thomas Edison was born in Milan, Ohio.
  • 1866 - He works at Western Union as a telegraph operator. (Note that he was fired a year later due to accident at workplace caused by one of his experiments.)
  • 1869 - Patents the electric vote recorder.
  • 1878 - Patents the first Phonograph.
  • 1879 - Patents electric lamp.
  • 1879 - Patents DC electricity distribution system.
  • 1880 - Establishs Edison Illumination Company.
  • 1892 - Thomas Edison establishs Edison General Electric after consolidating his several business interests.
  • 1931 - Thomas Edison Dies.

According to www.wikipedia, Thomas Edison's patents amounted to about a thousand in the United States. Among them includes:

  • phonograph
  • electric lamp
  • and many others

Thomas Edison is known to be a dedicated inventor who always find time to conduct his experiment.

His famous quotes are:

"Genius is one percent inspiration and ninety-nine percent perspiration."

"I have not failed. I've just found 10,000 ways that won't work."

"Many of life's failures are people who did not realize how close they were to success when they gave up."

"Our greatest weakness lies in giving up. The most certain way to succeed is always to try just one more time."

"Opportunity is missed by most people because it is dressed in overalls and looks like work."

"If we did all the things we are capable of, we would literally astound ourselves."
