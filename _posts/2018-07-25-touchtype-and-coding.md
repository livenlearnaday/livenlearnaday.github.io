You probably found this article by searching/googling the words "should I learn touchtyping / is it worth it to learn touchtyping / etc...". In my humble opinion, it is definitely worth it no matter what age you start as long as you are still spending time on the computer doing any typing.

The main benefit of touchtyping is you can concentrate on the idea/word formation (one less thing to thing about). In addition, once you start typing without looking at the keyboard, you can also improve your typing speed as you practise more. Other "benefits" are that you will feel really cool doing it and you can type in the dark/ in dim light/ or you can close your eyes while typing...

I started to practise touchtyping in the beginning of 2018 for at most 15min a day via [keybr](https://www.keybr.com/). Took me around 3 months (about maybe 5 days/week) to get acquinted with a-z keys (other people took less time than this, I'm just slow but we'll all get there eventually) and then I moved on to capital letters and the brackets which took like another 1 month. My typing speed is still slow (40 wpm) but I feel that I can get better and now I'm typing without looking at the keyboard which is really cool.

Despite being in the early stage of learning to program, I feel that touchtyping will definitely help in any typing done on the computer despite how slow the idea generation is. So if anyone is still looking to learn touchtyping skill, I'll definitely say go for it!
